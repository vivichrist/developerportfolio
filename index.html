Weird Wellington

Project Goals
  • Learn the basics of the Maya interface
  • Learn basic iterative scene construction and management
  • Learn basic modelling techniques and processes
  • Learn 2D and 3D compositional techniques
  • Learn basic rendering process
Description

  Take the city we live, remix, re-contextualise it to create a digital image that transforms or reinforces the mood and tone of the space. Wellington is a rich source of interesting places, spaces and architectural anomalies - In this project you will explore, observe and document a space in Wellington - from this reference material you’ll generate, manipulate and modify a 3D scene – resolving the composition into a ‘virtual photograph’ of this new space. This Project is divided into two distinct parts both of which have a hand-in requirement.

Moodboard for the first part.
Expedition

  Initially I roamed through Newtown taking photos of the Wellington Hospital area and found some compelling buildings and structures that seemed to have an interesting imagined story to them. I found various retro to modern architecture, the new look modern hospital and the old tarnished buildings around back, and the space between reminded me of themes like class war and cold war, berlin wall, apartheid etc. There were a lot of industrial facilities with big vents, funnels, load bearing beams, large molded concrete panels, spiralled metal chimney pipes in this industrial complex. One place that particularly took my interest was the generator building which looked a bit abandoned but was still in use.

Analysis

  I liked the monolithic tall vents and vented walls, made the whole building seem larger than it really was. There was also a service door hidden away between two towering concrete vent shafts that seemed very lonely and this became my focal point of a dystopian theme. This door that had barely any use. Faded and tagged it seemed like a door to a place I was restricted from going, in what could be a very exclusive separatist way. There was a sense of forgotten contemplation of pointless hopeless revelations, an industial autumn and so, I settled on a dystopian theme. Another building that also took my interest was an actually abandoned building on the outskirts with boarded up windows and cheaply built repetition along each floor, a boiler room in the basement, what seemed like a concrete moat of an old castle to distance the viewer for safety's sake, a no frill's ghetto projects like building, that didn't weather so well with time, the kind you would expect to find in the FPS game Half-life2.

Formation and reformation

  All of these buildings and structures would have a part to play in my 3D scene. The dilapidated building in the scene would become a representation of the poverty of the lower class. In contrast, I would need buildings that conveyed power and wealth as well as technological advancement. Dividing these two, a wall in the style of the generator building with guard towers, and the small faded red service door. I Listed down terms that were either intuitions, feelings or observations about the pictures of the actual space and did a search in google images and pinterest with the listed terms, gathering images of relevance that displayed the aesthetic I was going for. I looked at key searches:

  • Monolithic Architecture
  • Molded Concrete
  • Exclusion
  • Forgotten
  • Lonely
  • Gloom (lighting)
  • Wall
  • Industrial Lighting

  About a third of these images influenced my scenes buildings and structures, lighting, theme, texture etc. Guard towers are from the Israel Palestine wall images. The lighting is supposed to be gloomy with lots of light scattering and glow to give a cold inhibited feel. The directional light is dim to simulate a moon lit night. The molded concrete wall has been decorated to hide it's more sinister nature, a dystopian future of segregated class and values.
